## Project Overview

This project performs an end-to-end exploratory data analysis (EDA) and visual analytics on the IBM HR Attrition dataset to uncover the key drivers behind employee attrition. The goal is to help HR and management teams identify at-risk employee segments and take proactive retention measures.

## Key Findings

### 1. Department-Wise Attrition
The **Research & Development** department records the highest number of attrition cases among all departments. This suggests that R&D employees may be experiencing higher workload stress, limited growth opportunities, or misaligned expectations — factors that demand immediate HR attention.

### 2. Overtime and Attrition
Employees who work **overtime** show a significantly higher probability of attrition compared to those who do not. Excessive workload and poor work-life balance are major contributors to burnout, ultimately pushing employees to leave the organization.

### 3. Job Satisfaction and Attrition
Employees with **low job satisfaction scores** exhibit the highest attrition rates. This directly indicates that dissatisfied employees are unlikely to stay long-term, reinforcing the need for regular engagement surveys and role-fit assessments.

### 4. Marital Status and Attrition
- **Single employees** show the highest attrition rate among all marital status groups. Being early in their careers, single employees are more mobile, career-driven, and willing to switch companies for better compensation, skill growth, or learning opportunities.
- **Divorced employees** record the lowest attrition rate. This may reflect financial obligations and stability needs that make them less inclined to take the risk of changing jobs.

### 5. Salary Hike and Attrition
Employees who received a **very low percentage salary hike** are far more likely to leave the organization. Insufficient compensation growth signals a lack of recognition, directly impacting motivation and loyalty.

## Recommendations

- Implement **overtime monitoring policies** and enforce work-life balance measures.
- Conduct **quarterly job satisfaction surveys** with actionable follow-ups for low scorers.
- Design **targeted retention programs** for single employees and those in R&D.
- Review the **salary hike policy** to ensure competitive and merit-based compensation increments.
